Rough notes on something I've been thinking about...

## The shift from tool to collaborator

Traditional tools are passive. You push pixels, they move. AI tools push back. They have "opinions" (even if those opinions are statistical patterns).

This changes the creative dynamic. It's less like using Photoshop and more like working with a very fast, very literal junior designer.

## What I'm noticing

- The best outputs come from iteration, not first attempts
- Knowing what to ask for matters as much as the AI's capabilities

- Speed enables experimentation in ways we haven't fully explored
